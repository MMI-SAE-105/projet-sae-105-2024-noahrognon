Bionic (2024): The Future of Prosthetic Athletes

Bionic, directed by Afonso Poyart in 2024, envisions a future where prosthetics are no longer just tools for rehabilitation but enhancements that redefine human performance. In this futuristic universe, the Olympics are dominated by athletes using bionic prosthetics, creating a new standard of competition but raising ethical questions about sports fairness and the boundary between man and machine.

Film Synopsis

In Bionic, technological advancements in prosthetics have transformed the sports landscape. Athletes equipped with bionic limbs dominate competitions, and special Olympic Games are even dedicated to them. These prosthetics are so advanced that they allow athletes to surpass traditional human performance, sparking debates about fairness and the future of sports.

Film excerpt from Bionic (2024) where the athlete breaks a world record using a prosthetic

Man vs. Machine: An Uneven Competition?

“In a world where machines enhance human performance, where does sport end, and technology begin?”

The film raises critical questions: when do prosthetics become an unfair advantage? Can “normal” athletes still compete against opponents equipped with technologies that enhance strength, speed, and endurance? Bionic invites us to reflect on the limits of human augmentation and its social consequences.

Bionic (2024) Trailer

A Glimpse into the Future of Prosthetics

By exploring a future where prosthetics have become tools for athletic performance, Bionic transcends simple science fiction. The film questions the ethical implications of technology that could irreversibly alter our society. If prosthetics allow us to surpass human limits, how will we manage the inequalities they might create? Bionic is a visionary film that challenges us to rethink the relationship between humans and technology.
